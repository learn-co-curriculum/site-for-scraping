What makes The Flatiron School unique?

Good question. Ultimately, it comes down to 6 things. Teacher Quality, Logistics, Culture, Focus, Learning Philosophy and Teaching Philosophy.

General

Why shouldn't I just learn to code online?

We love online resources! They're great for getting started and can give you a solid foundation. But there's a lot more to being a productive developer than learning a specific language, from working in teams, to becoming part of the developer community, and that's what we want to focus on. As we like to say, you can get pretty good at guitar teaching yourself at home, but if you want to be part of a band, you need to play with people.

General

Do I need a computer?

We work on Macs with minimum specs. If you don't have one already, we can provide a loaner.

General

When should I apply?

Admissions for all programs are being done on a rolling basis, so the earlier you apply, the better.

General

Do I need any experience to apply?

Nope. We're just looking for great people with a strong desire to learn. Our students have ranged from having zero background to having 3 years of experience as a .NET developer who wanted to learn Ruby. The biggest common thread among our students is that each one has a genuine desire to be a great developers. So before you apply, make sure this is something you know you want to do. Try some tutorial. Read some books. Pair with a friend. If you’re 100% committed, nothing will get in your way.

Can I visit the campus?

Sure, signup to attend one of our rails meetups or iOS meetups.

Web Immersive

How much does the program cost?

Tuition for the Web Development Immersive is $15,000. We offer scholarships for students from underrepresented groups in tech such as women and minorities. Payment plans are available on a case-by-case basis for students who need help funding their education. Contact admissions@flatironschool.com for more information.

Web Immersive

Why Ruby?

The language you learn matters least. Programming is about abstractions and expressions: the mechanics of code are universal and exist in all modern languages. We teach Ruby because we love it. Thinking that your choice of language matters — especially as a beginner — is like thinking that you can only write poetry in English.

The beauty of poetry is in rhyme and meter, in metaphor and simile, in cadence and rhythm, not in the King's English. We teach Ruby and the Ruby ecosystem (which includes Rails) but you're really learning how to think, how to break problems down, how to express yourself, how to abstract ideas, and how to work together. We just do that through Ruby.

IOS Immersive

Are you teaching Swift?

Yup! We are covering both Objective-C and Swift for the upcoming semesters. A new language, doesn't mean we can just skip learning Objective-C. Its legacy is very strong still and our students must be comfortable in both languages.

Fellowship

What is the NYC Development Fellowship?

The NYC Web Development Fellowship is an 8-month program designed to help 18-26 year old New Yorkers without a 4-year degree get the programming skills necessary to launch careers as web developers.
