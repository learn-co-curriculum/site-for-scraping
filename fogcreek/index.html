Fog Creek

fellowship

Kickstarting women's careers in code.

January 5th to February 27th

Fog Creek will host the second class

of Fog Creek + Flatiron School Fellows—

a group of Flatiron School women

launching their careers in code.

We're so excited to announce our partnership with Fog Creek, and help kickstart women's careers in code.

The teams at Fog Creek, Trello, and Stack Exchange will support the Fellows with mentorship and resources while they begin their careers as developers, pairing each Fellow with an experienced engineer as a mentor. Fellows will hone their newly acquired skills with dedicated pairing time and full access to the host companies' offices.

Our joint goal is to help women begin their careers in code and land programming jobs they love; past Fellows are working at The Wall Street Journal, Intel, Refinery29 and Kickstarter.

Benefits for Fellows

  • A dedicated mentor who is an experienced member of their host company's engineering team
  • Events with the Fog Creek, Trello and Stack Exchange staff
  • Access to their host company's space, including
    meals, snacks and Wi-Fi
  • One mock interview, two pair programming sessions, and two one-on-one lunches per month

Benefits for Mentors

  • Build community and support diversity by helping to kick off a career
  • Sharpen your own skills through teaching and mentorship
  • Create relationships with a wider network of developers throughout the city and country

Fellows' Responsibilities

  • Schedule meetings with mentors and proactively follow up
  • Arrive at meetings with specific goals and questions for mentors to address
  • Blog once during the program about their experience
  • Work hard, write code, and eventually, pay it forward themselves

Mentors' Responsibilities

  • Attend a kickoff social to meet your mentee
  • Provide career mentorship, including one mock interview, two pair programming sessions, and two one-on-one lunches per month
  • Be communicative about your availability for meetings and questions
