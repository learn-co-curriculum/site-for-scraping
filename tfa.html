TFA - Flatiron School CS Education Fellows Program

We are thrilled to announce that The Flatiron School and Teach For America are teaming up to expand computer science education. The pilot Teach For America - Flatiron Computer Science Education Fellows program will help fill the growing demand for computer science education nationwide.

Details //

Launching Summer 2015, CS Education Fellows will:

  • Learn to code: A week-long intensive Coding Bootcamp at the Flatiron School in New York City.
  • Learn to teach code: Build skills, gain experience and receive a stipend while teaching high school students to code in the summer.
  • Teach code: Gain free access for their school to Flatiron's proprietary curriculum to deliver CS education programs in their school.

Requirements //

To be eligible, Teach For America alumni must:

  • Have completed their two-year commitment with TFA (alumni with additional teaching experience are also encouraged to apply)
  • Commit to teaching at least one more year
  • Agree to spearhead computer science initiatives (eg. electives, after-school, clubs, workshops, etc.) in their school.
  • Teachers from all subjects are encouraged to apply.
  • No prior experience is necessary, but a demonstrated interest in learning to code is a must.

Additional Details //

  • All teachers are required to complete 80-100 hours of online pre-work, one week of in-person training, and commit to teach for four to eight weeks as a co-instructor over the summer.
  • Fellows will receive a stipend of $5000 to $9000 depending on the length of their teaching commitment
  • Fellows will be responsible for transportation and housing throughout the program.
  • Applications will close on March 8th, 2015 at 11:59PM EST
